< header >

  • Represents the introductory content or a container for navigation links
  • Often appears at the top pof a page or section

< main >

  • Represents the main content of the document
  • There should only be one < main >

< section >

  • Represents a standalone section of content with a heading
  • commonly used to group related content

< article >

  • Represents independendent content that could be distributed or syndicated, like a blog post or news article

< aside >

  • Represents content related to the main content, such as sidebars or widgets

< footer >

  • Represents the footer of a page or a section
  • Often contains copyright, contact information or links
